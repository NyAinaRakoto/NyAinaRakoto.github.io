# Education

Before starting my Ph.D. in Geography at [Oklahoma State University, Stillwater, United States](https://go.okstate.edu/), I graduated with a Master's degree in Environmental Engineering, specialized in Soil and Water Conservation at the [Szent Istvan University, Hungary](https://en.uni-mate.hu/) with the [FAO Scholarship](https://www.fao.org/europe/news/detail/FAO---Hungarian-Government-Scholarship-2018-2019---Introduction-and-procedures/en). Before that, I also graduated with a Master's degree in Agronomy Engineering, specializing in Environment, Territory, and Development from the [Ecole Superieure des Sciences Agronomiques, Madagascar](https://essaforets.wordpress.com/). 

  
# Services and Leadership

Throughout my journey, I have been involved in different activities to give back to the communities. For example, I am a permanent member of [IkalaSTEM](https://www.ikalastem.org/) Chapter America, aiming to empower the next generation of women in STEM in Madagascar. While studying at Szent Istvan University, I was a mentor with the [Stipendium Hungaricum Mentorship Program](https://shmentor.hu/), helping new students in their integration in the university. While in Madagascar, I was an active member of [Malagasy Youth Biodiversity Network](https://gybninfo.wordpress.com/national-chapters/malagasy-youth-biodiversity-network/), leading various project to raise awareness on the loss of biodiversity. Activites included teaching elementary school students to plant trees. I was also a leader at [Teach for Madagascar](https://teach4madagascar.wordpress.com/), involved in different activities, such as teaching illiterate children.
